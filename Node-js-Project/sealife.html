Marine Life

Bottled nose Dolphin

We took a boat to go on multiple islands at Bocas del Toro and saw this dolphin playing around in a boat's wake.

Spotted Dolphin

We spotted these dolphins on a whale watching tour in Uvita thanks to Brown Boobie birds flying around them and catching the flying fish trying to escape the dolphins.

Olive Ridley Turtle

This baby Olive Ridley turtle was making its way towards the sea. It was collected as an egg on the beach among many other eggs by a costa rican who is giving them a better chance to survive. As soon as they hatch in his house, he comes to the beach and releases them. They have to find their way to the sea on their own so they get to develop their instincts before being in open water. Only one in 5000 survives.

Starfish Beach

We went to a place called Starfish Beach in Bocas del Toro. We got to swim around and spot many starfish laying around doing nothing.

Humpback Whale

During our whale watching tour in Uvita, we got to see a male humpback whale swimming around. Unfortunately, it didn't jump around for us, we only got to see its back and its tail.

Baby Turtle at sea

As soon as the baby Olive Ridley turtles managed to get in the water, they seemed way more comfortable. Some birds were flying near the water, probably feasting on some delicious baby turtles but we had to let them learn to survive.
